Community Guidelines

TL;DR: Respect. Privacy. Consent.

Participation in any activities organised by DDR Oslo requires that you act respectfully towards its members, the spaces we are in, and any other third parties.

By participating in this community, you agree to the community guidelines. You may be removed at any time if you fail to follow them.

Members

Treat your fellow humans as equals, deserving of the utmost respect.

This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Clear, non-violent communication;
  • Upholding privacy;
  • No harassment, discrimination or abuse;
  • Respect of boundaries;
  • Asking for consent before doing something that affects them.

Spaces

Respect the spaces where community gatherings take place, be them physical or virtual.

Physical Spaces

We are privileged to have spaces like Tilt which welcome us and our events. Show them the care and appreciation they deserve.

  • Do not scream or be rude. Keep your tone of voice and language appropriate to the space;
  • Keep it organised. Do not spread your belongings around the space;
  • Clean up after yourself;
  • Leave the space as you found it;
  • Treat staff with utmost respect;
  • Do not vandalise or cause damage.

Virtual Spaces

Being online and potentially anonymous is no excuse to behave inappropriately.

  • Use virtual spaces, like Signal, for their intended purposes;
  • Do not use the communication spaces to organise illegal and/or harmful activities;
  • Do not spread misinformation or disinformation, or AI content;
  • Do not vandalise the communication channels and virtual spaces (e.g.: no spam, DDOS attacks, ragebaiting, etc.).

Third Parties

There will be third parties in spaces such as Tilt. Respect them.

  • Others might want to use the DDR machines. Make sure they get their turn too;
  • Do not scream or be rude. Keep your tone of voice and language appropriate to the space;
  • Do not record or photograph third parties, even if they are only in the background;
  • All the respect afforded to members should be afforded to third-parties. (See Members)

Definitions

Harassment, discrimination or abuse

Harassment, discrimination or abuse can be, but are not limited to:

  • Taking pictures, videos or other forms of recording without someone's consent;
  • Unwanted flirting, touching, or sexual comments;
  • Repeatedly ignoring someone’s boundaries;
  • Making racist, sexist, ableist, transphobic, or otherwise discriminatory comments;
  • Mocking or dismissing someone’s access needs, culture, or way of communicating;
  • Gaslighting, manipulation, or abuse of social or organizational power.

These behaviors are unacceptable regardless of ill intent.

Privacy

It is the right of individuals to keep their personal information private. Respect it.

Do not disclose any personal information about someone in this group to a third party without the individual’s consent.

“Personal information” includes, but is not limited to:

  • Membership status in this community;
  • Pictures, videos, voice recordings, screenshots of messages, etc.;
  • Any personally identifiable information (name, e-mail, phone number, gender, sexuality, etc);
  • Information about interpersonal relationships (who their friends, partners, coworkers, etc. are).

Although GDPR is not designed for individuals, it is a reference for the minimum privacy standards to be upheld in this community.

Consent and Autonomy

Members of this community are free to draw boundaries wherever necessary. Respect them.

Examples of boundaries can be (but are not limited to):

  • “I only want to play DanceDanceRevolution. I don’t want to be friends.”
  • “Do not touch me.”
  • “Do not operate my side of the DDR cabinet.”
  • “Do not take pictures/videos where I may be in the frame.”
  • “I do not wish to disclose where I know X from.”
